Intervention Settings / Intervention Options

Option A and Option B cannot be interchanged. Please read the explanation to make sure that you define them correctly.

  • Explanation
  • HeartSteps Example

In the current method (Thompson sampling -- two options), there are two intervention options. Intervention options usually correspond to different buckets of messages or no message.

Here's how we distinguish Intervention Option A from Intervention Option B:

  1. The “main treatment effect” is defined as the change in the proximal outcome when Intervention option A is provided versus Intervention option B. In the Bayesian linear regression (see Eq.(3.1) in the tutorial), “Intervention=1” if Intervention option A is provided, and “Intervention=0” if Intervention option B is provided.
  2. In some predefined states (which you’ll define two pages from now), one may not wish to provide Intervention Option A. In those states, Intervention Option B will be provided. In other words, Intervention Option B is often less burdensome. In fact, it is often the “do nothing” option.

In HeartSteps, the goal of the JITAI is to encourage a person to walk more by sending activity suggestions. The options are to send an activity suggestion or not to send a suggestion. The treatment effect is the change in (log) next 30-minute step count when a suggestion is sent versus not sent.
