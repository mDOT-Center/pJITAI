General Settings / Personalization Method

This version of Thompson Sampling uses a Bayesian linear regression to model the relationship between the proximal outcome, states, and intervention options. This algorithm uses the data from each individual to learn and update that individual’s coefficients. This method determines which intervention option to deliver based on the posterior probability that the intervention option is most effective in the current state.

Key assumptions:

  • There are two intervention options.
  • The proximal outcome is continuous.
